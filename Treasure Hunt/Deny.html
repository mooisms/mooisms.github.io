Once in a while, regret settles in your stomach. “What if I had accepted the voice’s offer? Would I be richer? Would I have mattered more?” You don’t allow the thought to linger for long. You continue to live.

Years pass, and you grow much older. Time has slowed, and you are no longer searching for the things you once sought in your youth. Your body is not what it used to be, but you look at your life for what it was and feel gratitude. As you sit and reminisce, you remember the journey to find Eve—the woman and the child, the old man, and the voice. You laugh, as if you’ve just heard the funniest joke. Seeing it now through the lens of time, you realize you were never seeking wealth or fame—you were searching for meaning.

As you recall your interactions with each person you met, you think about the choices you made. You sit with the feeling and realize how deeply you treasure those moments.
